DPCSpell: A Denoising Transformer-based Detector-Purificator-Corrector Framework for Spelling Error Correction of Bangla and Resource-Scarce Indic Languages
Mehedi Hasan Bijoy, Nahid Hossain, Salekul Islam, Swakkhar Shatabda
Code / arXiv

The contributions of this article are summarized below:
• We propose a novel detector-purificator-corrector framework named DPCSpell, which is based on denoising transformers, for the SEC of Bangla and resource-scarce Indic languages such as Hindi and Telugu.
• We compare our method with state-of-the-art methods in different languages. It has become the new state-of-the-art method for Bangla SEC.
• A comprehensive comparison among rule-based, RNN-based, convolution-based, and transformer-based methods is performed for the SEC task.
• We introduce a method for developing a large-scale parallel corpus from scratch that overcomes the resource scarcity issue of left-to-right scripted languages. A large-scale parallel corpus for Bangla SEC is developed using our method and made publicly available, making Bangla no longer a low-resource language for the SEC task.

A Deep Learning Approach to Detecting Rice Leaf Diseases
Mehedi Hasan Bijoy, Nirob Hasan, Mithun Biswas, Suvodeep Mazumdar, Sifat Momen
(Code / Demonstration)

The contributions of this article are summarized below:
• We propose a lightweight dCNN architecture for rice leaf disease detection that outperforms previous works [8], [5], and [4] with 16, 811, and 152 times fewer parameters. It also outperforms [31] and [3].
• We compare our proposed model with seven benchmark architectures namely AlexNet, MobileNetV2, MobileNetV3, ResNet50, DenseNet121, ResNeXt50, and ShuffleNetV2, and achieve competitive performance with a much lower parameter size, hence, asymptotic complexity.
• We enhance the previous rice leaf disease dataset by collecting data, at least 95 unique RGB images, from the internet and manually annotating them by experts.
• We developed a crop health monitoring system for farmers which includes a website and an android app. Also, we developed an open API.
